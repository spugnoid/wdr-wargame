Section 9 — Close Assault
=========================

9.1  Declaration and Eligibility
--------------------------------


**9.1.1**  A unit may declare a close assault against an enemy unit in an adjacent hex. This costs 1 AP.

**9.1.2**  The assaulting unit must have a Move action available (M# > 0 and no MOVED or ASSAULT marker placed this turn).

**9.1.3**  A Suppressed or Pinned unit may not declare a close assault.

**9.1.4**  Close assault consists of up to three phases: Grenade Phase, Entry Fire Phase, and Melee Continuation (if both sides survive).

9.2  Voluntary Withdrawal
-------------------------


**9.2.1**  Before the Grenade Phase begins, the defending player may choose voluntary withdrawal.

**9.2.2**  Voluntary withdrawal is available only if: the defending unit is not Suppressed or Pinned; a clear withdrawal hex exists (not blocked, not occupied by enemy); and the defending unit has not already fired this turn (no FIRE marker present).

**9.2.3**  On voluntary withdrawal, the defender moves 1 hex away from the assaulting unit's approach direction. The attacker occupies the vacated hex unopposed. No combat occurs.

9.3  Grenade Phase
------------------


**9.3.1**  If the defender does not withdraw, the Grenade Phase begins. Both sides throw grenades simultaneously.

**9.3.2**  Attacker grenade attack: Roll 1d6+1d8+1d12 + attacker's G# value. Compare against defender's Defence + half the cover modifier (round down).

**9.3.3**  Defender grenade attack (simultaneous): Roll 1d6+1d8+1d12 + defender's G# value. Compare against attacker's Defence value. No cover modifier applies to the attacker — they are in the open approaching.

**9.3.4**  Apply results to both sides simultaneously using the standard Result Threshold Table (Rule 8.6).

**9.3.5**  Grenades halve the defender's cover modifier because they detonate inside the cover rather than against its exterior face.

**9.3.6**  If the defender is eliminated (Broken or Dispersed) in the Grenade Phase, the assault succeeds. The attacker enters the hex. The Entry Fire Phase does not occur.

**9.3.7**  If the attacker is eliminated in the Grenade Phase, the assault fails. The attacker is removed; the defender holds.

9.4  Entry Fire Phase
---------------------


**9.4.1**  If both sides survive the Grenade Phase, the attacker enters the hex. Both sides fire simultaneously at range 0.

**9.4.2**  Attacker fires using their dominant fire line rFP + range 0 bonus (+3).

**9.4.3**  Defender fires using their dominant fire line rFP + range 0 bonus (+3).

**9.4.4**  No cover modifier applies in the Entry Fire Phase — both sides are in the same position.

**9.4.5**  Apply results simultaneously using the standard Result Threshold Table.

**9.4.6**  If the defender is eliminated, the assault succeeds. If the attacker is eliminated, the assault fails and the attacker's counter is removed.

**9.4.7**  If both sides are eliminated simultaneously, both counters are removed. The hex is empty.

9.5  Melee Continuation
-----------------------


**9.5.1**  If both sides survive the Entry Fire Phase, the attacker remains in the hex. Melee continues into subsequent turns automatically — no AP is required to continue a melee already in progress.

**9.5.2**  At the start of each subsequent turn (during the Recovery Phase), units engaged in melee attempt recovery normally. Melee then resumes during the Action Phase.

**9.5.3**  During Melee Continuation turns, both sides fire simultaneously at range 0 with no cover modifier. Apply the range 0 bonus (+3 rFP) to both sides.

**9.5.4**  Melee continues until one side is eliminated, one side fails a morale check and withdraws, or one side voluntarily withdraws at the start of a turn (if eligible per Rule 9.2).

9.6  Melee Morale Check
-----------------------


**9.6.1**  After any melee turn in which a unit takes a Casualty result or worse, the affected unit must pass a morale check or attempt to withdraw.

**9.6.2**  Morale check: Roll 1d6 + unit Morale vs. the break threshold for the result suffered.

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: auto

   * - **Result Suffered**
     - **Break Threshold**
   * - Casualty
     - 8
   * - Casualty + Suppressed
     - 6
   * - Broken (last unit of side eliminated)
     - N/A — side eliminated


**9.6.3**  If the roll meets or exceeds the break threshold, the unit holds. Melee continues.

**9.6.4**  If the roll is below the break threshold, the unit attempts withdrawal (see Rule 9.7).

9.7  Withdrawal and Routing
---------------------------


**9.7.1**  A unit that fails a melee morale check attempts to withdraw from the melee hex.

**9.7.2**  The withdrawing unit moves 1 hex away from the melee hex in a direction away from the enemy. Place SUPPRESSED marker on the withdrawing unit.

**9.7.3**  If no clear withdrawal hex is available (blocked terrain, enemy units in all adjacent hexes), the withdrawing unit is Dispersed instead of withdrawing. The counter moves to the DISPERSED zone of the Casualty Track in its current state. A serialised DISPERSED marker is placed in the current hex.

**9.7.4**  After a successful withdrawal, the melee ends. The opposing unit occupies the hex.
