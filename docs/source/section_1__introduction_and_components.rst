Section 1 — Introduction and Components
=======================================

1.1  Game Overview
------------------


With Deepest Regret... is a free, complete hex and counter wargame system representing squad-level infantry and armoured combat during World War II. The title takes its name from the universal opening of death notification letters sent to families in every nation that fought the war — German, Soviet, American, British, and all others. The ellipsis is intentional: the sentence that follows was always the same, and the men on these counters are that sentence.

Each counter represents a single squad, weapon team, vehicle, or leader. The game operates at two scales: tactical (40 yards per hex, squad actions) and operational (250 yards per hex, platoon and company actions). Rules for both scales share a common resolution system.

The game is designed to reward historically accurate tactics. Cover, fire superiority, coordinated movement, and command and control produce results consistent with documented WWII combat outcomes. Gamey shortcuts — particularly the skulking behaviour common in other squad-level games — are eliminated by design.

With Deepest Regret... is released free of charge as a complete open system. It includes rules, counter sheets, markers, and maps. It is designed for physical play and digital platforms including Vassal. The complete component set — rules, counters, and a large inventory of generated maps — makes the game fully accessible without cost.

The game does not glorify war. The ordinary soldiers of every nation were not evil — they were young men doing what their countries asked. The tragedy is universal. This system models it with accuracy and respects what it cost.

1.3  Definitions and Terminology
--------------------------------


The following terms are used throughout this document. Each term is defined here on first use and applied consistently.

**Unit —**  Any counter representing a combat element: squad, weapon team, vehicle, or leader.

**Counter —**  The physical cardboard piece representing a unit or marker on the map.

**Hex —**  A single hexagonal space on the map. All distances are measured in hexes.

**Stack —**  Two or more counters occupying the same hex.

**Active player —**  The player currently spending Action Points during an impulse.

**Non-active player —**  The player not currently spending Action Points; may spend Reaction Points.

**rFP —**  Raw Firepower. The base firepower value printed on a counter before range falloff is applied.

**Effective rFP —**  Firepower after range falloff and terrain modifiers have been applied.

**Resolution FP —**  The value looked up on the Resolution Strip after summing effective rFP across a fire group. Used as the bonus added to the dice roll.

**Falloff —**  The reduction in rFP as range increases, governed by the ⬡h and -f values on the counter.

**D3 —**  Roll 1d6, halve, round up: results 1–3. Used for rout movement and mortar dispersion distance.

**Fire group —**  All firing units sharing the same ⬡h interval value, resolved as a single attack. Effective rFP is calculated per unit and summed (Rule 8.3.2); units sharing a hex may sum rFP and -f values first (Rule 8.3.3).

**Cover modifier —**  A bonus added to the defender's Defence value based on terrain occupied.

**Intervening terrain —**  Terrain hexes that the line of fire passes through between firer and target, reducing effective rFP.

**CI —**  Combat Ineffective. A unit that has been eliminated from play for the scenario.

**Broken —**  A unit rendered CI by ranged fire. Counter removed from map.

**Dispersed —**  A unit rendered CI by close assault or morale failure. Counter moves to the DISPERSED zone of the Casualty Track. A serialised DISPERSED marker is placed in the hex to indicate men are physically present.

**Suppressed —**  A status condition reducing a unit's movement and fire effectiveness.

**Pinned —**  A status condition preventing movement and severely degrading fire effectiveness.

**Exposure —**  The condition of a unit that has moved and fired in the same impulse, or fired from open ground a second time. Exposed units may be targeted by opportunity fire.

**AP —**  Action Points. The resource controlling how many units the active player may activate per turn.

**RP —**  Reaction Points. The resource controlling how many times the non-active player may respond to enemy actions per turn.

**BTV —**  Base Training Value. A quality multiplier representing the unit's training level at formation.

**EM —**  Experience Modifier. A campaign multiplier reflecting combat experience gained or lost.

**MM —**  Morale Modifier. A campaign multiplier reflecting unit morale state.

**VISIBLE —**  A unit whose counter is placed openly on the map, fully identified by the opponent.

**HIDDEN —**  A unit whose counter is on the hidden information chart under cover, represented on the map by a blind marker.

**FIXED —**  A unit not yet on the map whose position is recorded on a scenario record sheet. No blind marker until it activates or is contacted.

**Blind marker —**  A map token representing a hidden unit. Shows size category but not unit identity or contents.

**Dummy marker —**  A blind marker with no unit behind it. Used to create uncertainty about a hidden unit's true position.

**CONTACT marker —**  A marker placed at a unit's last known position when it goes hidden mid-play. Advances through fresh, recent, and cold states before removal.

**CON —**  Concealment value. A modifier reflecting how hard a unit is to spot based on terrain and behaviour.

**OBS —**  Observation value. A modifier reflecting how effectively a unit detects hidden enemies. Primarily a leader stat.

**Spot roll —**  A roll to detect a hidden unit: 1d6 + OBS modifiers vs CON modifiers. Success reveals the target.

**Careless movement —**  A declared movement type granting +1 hex of movement at the cost of -2 CON and a free enemy spot attempt.

**Free hidden impulse —**  A movement phase granted immediately when a unit goes hidden, outside the normal AP economy, used to reposition the blind marker and spawn dummies.

**Morale check —**  A roll made when a unit experiences a stress trigger. Roll 1d6 + Morale vs break threshold. Failure produces a Break or Rout result.

**Break —**  A psychological CI result. Unit removed to BROKEN zone of Casualty Track without necessarily taking physical casualties. Marked with white CI cause marker.

**Rout —**  A fleeing morale failure. Unit moves D3 hexes away from nearest visible enemy each activation. ROUTING marker placed. Can rally at Recovery Phase.

**Cascade —**  The contagion effect when a unit Breaks or Routs. All friendly units within 2 hexes with LOS to the breaking unit must immediately check morale at threshold 5.

**Force Morale value —**  The threshold of CI and routing units at which a force morale check is triggered. Calculated as unit count × force factor at scenario start.

**Force morale check —**  A roll made when CI and routing units reach the Force Morale value. Roll 1d6 + highest functional leader CMD vs 8. Failure ends the scenario.

**Indirect fire —**  Fire that does not require LOS to the target hex. Mortars and artillery. Targets a hex coordinate, not a specific unit.

**Fire mission —**  A single indirect fire attack. Requires a sealed slip written at the time of calling. Arrives after a delay determined by weapon type and mode.

**Sealed slip —**  A folded paper record written when a fire mission is called. Contains target hex and arrival timing. Cannot be modified after writing. Revealed on arrival.

**Dispersion —**  The deviation of an indirect fire round from its intended target hex. Determined by accuracy roll — distance in hexes (D3) and direction (1d6) from further rolls.

**Forward observer (FO) —**  Any friendly unit with LOS to the target hex that calls an indirect fire mission. Leaders add OBS rating to mortar accuracy when acting as FO.

**AMO —**  Ammunition. The number of fire missions a mortar can fire per scenario. Base value printed on counter. Secret bonus added at setup. Extended ammunition table used past base value.

**Registered target —**  A hex pre-designated at scenario setup as a mortar fire target. Fires with reduced delay and no accuracy penalty.

**SMOKE marker —**  A marker placed when smoke rounds land. Numbered 1–3 indicating dissipation step. Each step reduces concealment and intervening fire penalty.

**AV —**  Armour Value. Effective armour protection in millimetres, already resolved at design time (slope, cast/rolled deficiency, high-hardness, flaw, and face-hardening corrections all folded in). Every vehicle prints separate AV-vs-Capped and AV-vs-Tungsten values for each of its Hull and Turret profiles — see Rule 17.2. HEAT does not get its own printed AV; it uses the universal HEAT reference table (Rule 17.2.7) applied to the vehicle's raw armour thickness instead.

**Hull / Turret profile —**  The two independently-printed armour profiles on a split-AV vehicle counter. Which profile a shot resolves against is determined by the Gunnery Roll (Rule 18.1a), not by the attacker's choice.

**PEN —**  Penetration value. A gun or weapon's ability to defeat armour, expressed as 0°-equivalent millimetres at a small set of range bands printed on the counter (Rule 17.3) rather than a single value with falloff notation. A gun may print more than one PEN line if it historically fired more than one ammunition nature (e.g. Capped and Tungsten).

**TRAV —**  Traverse rating. The number of hex sides a vehicle's gun can cover from its current facing in one fire action. TRAV 3 = any target. TRAV 0 = front hex only (casemate).

**Arc —**  The facing zone (Front, Side, or Rear) of whichever profile (Hull or Turret) a shot resolves against, per the Gunnery Roll. Determines which AV value applies.

**Gunnery Roll —**  The roll made whenever a vehicle-mounted gun fires at another vehicle, resolving in a single roll whether the round hits at all and, if it does, whether it strikes the target's Hull or Turret profile. Rule 18.1a. Always the first step of vehicle fire resolution.

**Crew Quality —**  A firing vehicle's gunnery skill tier (Elite/Veteran/Regular/Green/Militia), derived from its own Morale value (Rule 18.1a.2). Caps the maximum hit probability the Gunnery Roll can achieve.

**Miss Threshold —**  The lowest 1d6+1d8+1d12 roll that avoids a miss on the Gunnery Roll, printed on the firing vehicle's counter per range band.

**Hull Threshold —**  The lowest Gunnery Roll that strikes the Hull profile outright. A roll between the Miss Threshold and the Hull Threshold strikes the Turret instead.

**Crossing target —**  A target vehicle that moved this turn into a position where the firer's line of fire falls in its Side arc, rather than Front or Rear. Harder to hit than a stationary or directly closing/receding target (Rule 18.1a.6).

**TRAVERSED marker —**  Placed on a turreted vehicle when it fires at a target outside its hull's front hexside, showing the turret's actual current facing independent of the hull's facing arrow. Rule 17.5.5.

**Automatic penetration —**  When effective PEN ≥ AV + 3. Round penetrates without a dice roll.

**Contested penetration —**  When effective PEN ≥ AV but PEN < AV + 3. Roll 1d6 to determine penetration outcome.

**Bounce —**  When effective PEN < AV - 3. Round deflects with no effect.

**Shatter Gap —**  Optional/advanced rule (18.2a). A kinetic round that badly over-penetrates a plate can shatter its own nose before completing penetration and fail, despite nominally clearing AV by a comfortable margin.

**Schürzen —**  Standoff skirt armour fitted to a vehicle's Hull or Turret Side arc, effective against HEAT specifically (Rule 18.2b). No effect against kinetic rounds.

**Bail-out —**  Vehicle crew abandoning their vehicle under sustained fire or morale failure. CREW counter placed in hex. Vehicle remains as ABANDONED counter.

**Buttoned up —**  Vehicle Pinned status. Crew closes hatches — limited visibility, -2 OBS, cannot spot hidden units.

**Overrun —**  Vehicle moving into an enemy infantry hex. Triggers defensive AT fire and morale check before close combat.

**CREW counter —**  A small counter representing vehicle crew without a vehicle. Can crew friendly or captured vehicles. Moves using infantry routing rules when bailed out.

**Deliberate targeting —**  A sniper declaring a specific unit as the target of fire rather than a hex. Full result thresholds apply — long range cap exempt.

**Sniper detection roll —**  A roll made after a sniper fires to determine if the firing position is revealed. Roll 1d6 + OBS modifiers vs sniper CON.

**Breach —**  A cleared path through an obstacle or minefield created by an engineer unit. Marked with BREACH marker. All friendly units may use the breach freely.

**Demolition —**  An engineer action destroying a terrain feature. Bridge, building, fortification, or road junction. Automatic success given time; roll under fire.

**Burning marker —**  Placed when a hex is hit by flamethrower or catches fire. Units in hex take rFP 3 attack each turn. Persists until extinguished or 3 turns pass.

**Capability marker —**  An icon on an engineer counter's support band showing available specialist capabilities: DEMO, BRH, MINE, FLAM, BRDG, FORT. Covered with EXPENDED strip when used.

--------------------

     - Upper centre
     - Nation, type, and year bracket (e.g., GREN 43)
   * - Action values
     - Upper right
     - M# F# G# — Move allowance, Fire rate, Grenade value
   * - Fire lines
     - Centre
     - One to three lines of rFP ⬡h -f with weapon class icon

     - Morale value used for recovery and morale checks
   * - Support weapon band
     - Bottom strip
     - Coloured band showing integral support weapon stats


Rear face of each counter shows the reduced strength version of the unit with independently calculated stats. See Section 3.2.

1.5  Counter Design Philosophy
------------------------------


With Deepest Regret... is designed so that a player can resolve the majority of game actions using only the counters and the player aid card. The rulebook defines the system. The counter carries the data.

**1.5.1**  Information on the counter — not in a table:  Every fire line encodes base firepower, falloff interval, and falloff loss — the complete information needed to calculate effective fire at any range without a lookup. Vehicle counters carry all three armour facing values and the complete penetration curve. Leaders carry all four command stats. No chart lookup is required for routine combat resolution.

**1.5.2**  What remains on the player aid card:  The resolution strip (rFP to Resolution FP conversion for combined fire), result thresholds (memorised after a few games), terrain cover modifiers, and vehicle damage tables. These are the minimum necessary lookups. Everything else is on the counter.

**1.5.3**  Counter size:  Counters are produced at 0.75 inch (19mm) or 1 inch (25.4mm) — significantly larger than the wargame industry standard of 0.5 inch (12.7mm). The larger size makes all counter information readable without magnification. At 1 inch a counter has approximately four times the area of a standard counter — sufficient to display multiple fire lines, all action values, armour facings, and defence and morale values in comfortable reading size.

**1.5.4**  SVG format — resolution independent:  All counters and maps are generated in Scalable Vector Graphics (SVG) format. SVG is mathematically defined rather than pixel-based, meaning the same file prints at any physical size with no quality loss. A counter designed at nominal size prints identically at 0.75 inch or 1 inch. Maps scale to any paper size. The same files are used for physical print-and-play and digital platforms such as Vassal without modification.

**1.5.5**  Map and counter scale matching:  Map hex size must accommodate counter size for comfortable stacking. Recommended hex sizes:

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: auto

   * - **Counter size**
     - **Recommended hex size**
     - **A3 sheet capacity (approx)**
     - **Notes**
   * - 0.5 inch (12.7mm) — standard
     - 9/16 inch (14.3mm)
     - ~26 × 18 hexes
     - Industry standard — cramped stacking
   * - 0.75 inch (19mm) — recommended
     - 15/16 inch (24mm)
     - ~17 × 12 hexes
     - Comfortable stacking of 2–3 counters
   * - 1 inch (25.4mm) — large format
     - 1.25 inch (32mm)
     - ~13 × 10 hexes
     - Generous, very readable, best for older eyes


**1.5.6**  Free and open release:  With Deepest Regret... is released free of charge as a complete open system. Rules, counter sheets, markers, and a large inventory of generated maps are all freely available. The system is designed to make squad-level hex and counter wargaming accessible without the financial barrier of commercial systems. All components can be printed at home or through a print service.
